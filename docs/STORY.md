# Hollow Pines — The Search

*Five friends, three nights, and the thing they came to prove is real.*

---

## The Expedition

Five friends. Three nights. One shared obsession most of the people in their lives had long since
stopped asking about.

They weren't scientists on a grant — not really. They were a cryptozoologist, a wildlife photographer,
a park ranger, an audio engineer, and a field medic who had found each other the way believers do: in
the comment threads and the meetups and the long forum posts that stay alive at two in the morning. What
they had in common was Bigfoot — the quiet, faintly embarrassing certainty that the woods of the Pacific
Northwest were hiding something real, and that everyone who mattered had simply stopped looking.

So they went looking. Together. Three nights in Hollow Pines with everything they could carry and one
plain, impossible goal: find it, get it on record, and bring proof back to civilization — the clean,
undeniable kind that a hundred years of blurry, arguable nothing had never managed.

---

## Arrival

> *This is the game's opening — a short first-person brief as you ride in, before the first night begins.*

The drive in is the last easy part. Pavement gives way to gravel, gravel to a rutted fire road, and the
road itself gives up where the trees close ranks. Somewhere back there the last bar of cell signal blinks
out — you watch it go — and then it's just the van, the headlights, and a wall of old-growth pine going
copper, then grey, then black in the mirrors. They leave the van at the ranger trailhead. From here,
everything is on foot.

For as long as anyone has written things down — and long before that, in the stories told and retold
around other fires — the northwestern woods have spoken back. Hikers hear it before they ever see it: a
roar that belongs to nothing in the field guide, rolling in from somewhere out in the dark. In the
morning they find the prints, too large and too deep and too far apart to laugh off. And every so often,
snagged on a branch at a height no bear could reach, a tuft of coarse hair no lab has ever quite been
able to name.

Centuries of it. And in all those centuries, not one person has brought home proof that couldn't be
explained away. That's the part that keeps them up at night — not that it might be real, but that it has
stayed hidden this well, this long, like it *chose* to.

Tonight the goal is simple, and it fits inside a lens: get Bigfoot on camera. Real footage — steady,
close, lit, unmistakable — enough of it, pooled across the team and across the three nights, to end the
argument for good.

> *Filming is the only evidence mechanic today. Future versions may add other ways to gather proof —
> plaster casts, hair samples, audio recordings — see `docs/CHARACTER_FUNC_DEV.md` and the roadmap.*

"Sundown in twenty minutes," said Wren, checking the sky the way only someone who has been genuinely lost
checks it — not for weather, for the time she can't get back. "After that, the forest belongs to whatever
lives in it."

They split the gear. They split the dark. They turned on their flashlights.

Behind them, where the firelight didn't reach, the ferns moved against the wind — and went still.

## The Five Searchers

Each match, if you play a Searcher you're **randomly assigned one of the five** — so every night is a
different cut of the team and no two runs play the same. All five share the same core kit: move, sprint,
film, revive a downed teammate, dazzle Bigfoot with a sustained light, and vault a fallen log. Their
**specialty** just makes each one better at a single thing.

> *Specialties are design intent — not all of them are wired up yet. See `docs/CHARACTER_FUNC_DEV.md`
> for what exists today, what's planned, and the implementation plan.*

### 🔬 Dr. Mara Okonkwo — *Lead Cryptozoologist*
Calm, exacting, the one who keeps the team from bolting. **Specialty: Analysis.** Collects and
casts evidence faster, and can confirm whether a find is "the real thing" — saving the team
from wasting the night on bear scratches and elk tracks.

### 📷 Eli Vance — *Wildlife Photographer*
Has photographed grizzlies at six feet and lived. Believes the lens is braver than he is.
**Specialty: The Photo.** His camera captures evidence at longer range and its flash can briefly
**stun and reveal** Bigfoot — but it screams *here I am* to the whole forest.

### 🥾 Wren Castellano — *Park Ranger & Tracker*
Knows these woods better than the maps do; lost a friend to them once and never said so.
**Specialty: Tracking.** Sees fresh footprints and disturbed brush glowing faintly in the dark,
moves quietly, and can mark Bigfoot's trail for the team.

### 🎙️ Theo Park — *Audio Engineer & Podcaster*
Came for a season finale; staying for his life. **Specialty: Sound.** His parabolic mic hears
Bigfoot's movement and breathing from far off, giving the team early warning — and a recording
counts as a full piece of evidence.

### 🩹 Sam Reyes — *Survivalist & Field Medic*
Ex‑search‑and‑rescue. Treats panic like a wound to be dressed. **Specialty: Endurance.** Revives
downed teammates faster, carries spare flashlight batteries to share, and keeps a cooler head
(more stamina) when the roaring starts.

## The Sixth Player — Bigfoot

Not a villain. A **resident**. It was here before the trailhead, before the fire road, before the county
that keeps such careful files on the ones who go missing — here before the maps, before the language the
maps are written in. The stories the searchers grew up on, traded at midnight and half-believed, are only
the latest telling of stories their grandparents heard, and theirs before them: the tall thing, the quiet
watcher at the edge of the light. Generation after generation has passed the myth down without ever laying
a hand on the truth of it. It has watched them do it.

It does not hate them; it does not think in the shape of hate. It simply does not want to be *found* — and
a hundred years and more of being looked for has taught it, with the patience of something that has all
the time in the world, exactly how the looking works. It knows light means people. It knows people in a
line means a search. It knows that if it waits in the dark long enough, the lights always, always come
apart.

Tonight, for the first time, the lights might be smart enough to stay together.

---

## Three Nights

Each night runs from **9 p.m. to 7 a.m.** — ten hours of dark to hunt in, or to survive. Three nights
running. The searchers win by banking enough clean footage before the third dawn; Bigfoot wins by living
to see it.

Dawn comes at seven. Not everyone out here is going to be there to watch it come up.
