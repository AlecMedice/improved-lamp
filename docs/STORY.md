# Hollow Pines — The Search

*A short story, and the five who go looking.*

---

## The Hollow Pines Expedition

The road into Hollow Pines ends where the asphalt gives up and the gravel takes over, and
the gravel gives up where the trees close in. They left the van at the ranger trailhead with
the last bar of cell signal and a sun already going copper behind the ridgeline.

Eleven hikers had vanished from this forest over forty years. The official reports said
*exposure*, *misadventure*, *lost*. The unofficial ones — the ones traded on forums at two
in the morning — said something else. They said *tall*. They said *fast*. They said
*it watched the camp from the treeline and did not blink.*

Dr. Mara Okonkwo did not believe in monsters. She believed in **data**. That was why she
had spent her own grant money to put five people and four hundred pounds of equipment at the
edge of the Hollow Pines National Forest on the one night of the year the locals swore the
woods "went quiet." Get the footprint cast. Get the fur. Get one clean photograph, one clean
recording, and the thing the believers had never managed in a hundred years of looking — get
it *home* and put it in front of people who couldn't laugh it away.

"Sundown in twenty minutes," said June, checking the sky the way only someone who'd been lost
in real wilderness checks the sky. "After that, the forest belongs to whatever lives in it."

They split the gear. They split the dark. They turned on their flashlights.

Behind them, where the firelight didn't reach, the ferns moved against the wind — and went
still.

## The Five Searchers

Each is a playable Searcher with a light asymmetric specialty. All can move, sprint, carry
evidence, and revive a downed teammate; their tool just makes them *better* at one thing.

### 🔬 Dr. Mara Okonkwo — *Lead Cryptozoologist*
Calm, exacting, the one who keeps the team from bolting. **Specialty: Analysis.** Collects and
casts evidence faster, and can confirm whether a find is "the real thing" — saving the team
from wasting the night on bear scratches and elk tracks.

### 📷 Eli Vance — *Wildlife Photographer*
Has photographed grizzlies at six feet and lived. Believes the lens is braver than he is.
**Specialty: The Photo.** His camera captures evidence at longer range and its flash can briefly
**stun and reveal** Bigfoot — but it screams *here I am* to the whole forest.

### 🥾 June "Junebug" Halloran — *Park Ranger & Tracker*
Knows these woods better than the maps do; lost a friend to them once and never said so.
**Specialty: Tracking.** Sees fresh footprints and disturbed brush glowing faintly in the dark,
moves quietly, and can mark Bigfoot's trail for the team.

### 🎙️ Theo Park — *Audio Engineer & Podcaster*
Came for a season finale; staying for his life. **Specialty: Sound.** His parabolic mic hears
Bigfoot's movement and breathing from far off, giving the team early warning — and a recording
counts as a full piece of evidence.

### 🩹 Sam Reyes — *Survivalist & Field Medic*
Ex‑search‑and‑rescue. Treats panic like a wound to be dressed. **Specialty: Endurance.** Revives
downed teammates faster, carries spare flashlight batteries to share, and keeps a cooler head
(more stamina) when the roaring starts.

---

## The Sixth Player — Bigfoot

Not a villain. A **resident**. Older than the trail signs, older than the road. It does not hate
them; it simply does not want to be *found*, and forty years of being looked for has taught it
exactly how the looking works. It knows light means people. It knows people in a line means a
search. It knows that if it waits in the dark long enough, the lights always, always come apart.

Tonight, for the first time, the lights might be smart enough to stay together.

Dawn is at 6:14 a.m. Someone is not going to see it.
